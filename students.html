When Higher Education Costs Hurt Louisiana’s Students…

Where Was Bill?

Congressman Cassidy has voted to cut Pell Grants for Louisiana students by 25 percent after Governor Jindal slashed more than $700 million from Louisiana's higher education budget over the last five years.

Congressman Cassidy voted to cut Pell Grants by 25 percent and stayed silent as more than $700 million was cut from Louisiana’s higher education budget by Gov. Jindal, causing tuition to rise 40-50 percent. In Louisiana, there are more than 600,000 individuals with student loan debt that averages $22,000 per person. Tuitions have gone up 40-50 percent as Congressman Cassidy has stood by Gov. Jindal cutting $700 million from the higher education budget. Congressman Cassidy has refused to support Mary Landrieu's plan to make college more affordable.

Mary Landrieu Led the Fight to Make College more Affordable for Louisiana Students

Mary Landrieu launched her Passport to the Middle Class initiative to give everyone a fair shot to earn a Passport to the Middle Class. It would reduce interest rates for responsible loan borrowers and restore the purchasing power of the Pell Grant by doubling it.

Where Was Bill?

When Gov. Jindal cut higher education more than any other state in the country, he stayed silent.
