When Higher Education Costs Hurt Louisiana’s Students…

Where Was Bill?

Congressman Cassidy Remained Silent as Gov. Jindal Cut $700 million from Louisiana’s Higher Education Budget, Voted to Cut Pell Grants by 25%

In Louisiana, there are more than 600,000 individuals with student loan debt that averages $22,000 per person. Tuitions have gone up 40-50 percent as Congressman Cassidy has stood by Gov. Jindal cutting $700 million from the higher education budget. Congressman Cassidy has refused to support Sen. Landrieu's plan to make college more affordable.

Mary Landrieu Led the Fight to Make College more Affordable for Louisiana Students

Sen. Landrieu launched her Passport to the Middle Class initiative to give everyone who works hard and plays by the rules a fair shot to earn a Passport to the Middle Class. It would reduce interest rates for responsible loan borrowers and restore the purchasing power of the Pell Grant by doubling it.

Where Was Bill?

When Gov. Jindal cut higher education more than any other state in the country, he stayed silent.
