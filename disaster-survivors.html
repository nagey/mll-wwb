When Hurricane Isaac Devastated Families and Businesses in Louisiana…

Where Was Bill?

Congressman Cassidy voted against $100 Million to rebuild after Hurricane Isaac

The parishes worst-hit during Hurricane Isaac have been waiting for a levee to protect them for 40 years. The West Shore Lake Pontchartrain project (“West Shore Levee” project) began with an authorization by Congress in July 1971. Like many U.S. Army Corps of Engineer projects, negotiations between local, state and federal officials continued without meaningful progress. Major flooding problems occurred in this area from Hurricanes Betsy (1965), Juan (1985), Rita (2005), and Gustav and Ike (2008).

Unfortunately, on August 28, 2012, Hurricane Isaac damaged 59,000 homes, most of which flooded, and showed how badly this area needs the West Shore Levee. The town of LaPlace was devastated.

Sen. Landrieu Fought & Delivered Disaster Relief and Levee Funding

In December 2012, the Senate passed a disaster funding bill that included a provision authored by Sen. Landrieu that approved the construction of the West Shore Levee. The bill also included $2.9 billion for construction of the West Shore Levee and other projects. Both Sens. Vitter and Landrieu supported this language.

The bill, as written by Sen. Landrieu as Chair of Homeland Security Appropriations, also lowered the cost to local governments from 35% to 10%, saving local governments hundreds of millions of dollars. The bill also made levee construction vastly more affordable for local governments letting them repay their share of the costs over a 30-year period. This bill passed the Senate by a vote of 62-32 (10 Republicans voted yes).

Where Was Bill?

When this disaster funding moved through the House, Congressman Cassidy was asked whether Louisiana should get $100 million in Isaac funding, he said no.

When leaders in the Republican Party were stripping the approval and funding for the levee, where was Congressman Cassidy to protect the people of his own District?
